The Nexus Framework

Plain-English Guide · How the System Works · Start Here

🧠

Think of Nexus as a team of specialists — not a single chatbot.

Most AI tools are one person doing everything. Nexus works more like a company with departments. There's a head agent (Nexus) who receives every task, decides who's best suited for it, and hands it off. Specialist agents handle their piece. Then the results come back, get reviewed, and get delivered to you. No single agent is doing everything — and that's the point. When one part fails, the rest keep running.

The 3-Layer Architecture — Why It's Built This Way
Layer 1
Directives
"The Instructions"

Written playbooks that tell the agents exactly what to do, in what order, and what to do when something goes wrong. They live as simple text files.

Example:"Every morning, pull the last 24 hours of email. Flag anything from a VIP contact. Summarize action items. Do not send replies without review."
Layer 2
Orchestration
"The Brain"

This is the AI making decisions. It reads the directives, figures out the right sequence of steps, assigns work to specialist agents, and handles anything unexpected.

Example:"User asked for a morning brief. Step 1: pull email (Email Agent). Step 2: pull calendar (Cal Agent). Step 3: assemble and deliver."
Layer 3
Execution
"The Workers"

Deterministic Python scripts that actually do the work. They connect to Gmail, Google Calendar, Notion, and other tools. They're reliable and testable — no guessing.

Example:run_triage.py connects to Gmail, reads 50 emails, applies labels, returns a structured list back to the brain.

Why not just ask ChatGPT to do everything?

One AI does steps 1→5 vs Specialist per step
90% accuracy × 5 steps = 59% final success rate
Each time an AI makes a decision, there's a chance it's slightly wrong. Chain 5 decisions together and errors multiply. Nexus breaks work into small, auditable steps with deterministic code doing the reliable parts — so errors don't compound.
Nexus pushes complex logic into tested scripts. The AI focuses on decision-making, not data fetching.

Real-world analogy

📋 Directives = Your company's SOPs and job descriptions
🧠 Orchestration = The manager who reads the SOP and tells people what to do
⚙️ Execution = The employees who actually do the specific task
🗂️ Skills = The specific techniques each employee knows
📬 Escalation = When a decision is above the manager's pay grade — it comes to you

How a Task Actually Moves Through the System
Example task:
"Give me my morning brief."
STEP 1
🏛️ Nexus
Receives request. Identifies it as a "morning brief" task. Routes to Vertex.
›
STEP 2
📬 Vertex
Runs run_triage.py. Pulls last 24hrs of email. Applies triage labels.
›
STEP 3
📬 Vertex
Runs calendar script. Pulls today's events. Flags any prep needed.
›
STEP 4
🏛️ Nexus
Receives both outputs. Checks quality — is this complete? Is anything missing?
›
STEP 5
✍️ Quill
Assembles the brief. Formats it cleanly. Prepares it for delivery.
›
STEP 6
📬 Delivered
Brief lands in email or Notion. You read it in under 2 minutes.
Skills — The Specific Techniques Each Agent Knows
Skills 1–7

Task Management

How Nexus receives, validates, clarifies, and plans every request. These run on every task, no exceptions. Think of them as the intake process before any work starts.

Skills 8–19

Domain Work

Specialist capabilities — real estate research, email triage, calendar management, lead scraping. Each skill maps to a directive file and a Python script.

Skills 20–23

Quality Control

How the system decides if the output is good enough. Is it done? Does it need another pass? Should it escalate? These prevent the system from delivering garbage confidently.

What Nexus Is and Isn't

Nexus is NOT...

A chatbot you talk to casually
A replacement for your judgment on important decisions
A magic fix that works without setup
Something that improvises when it doesn't know the answer
One AI model doing everything
A system that hides failures from you

Nexus IS...

A structured team of specialists — each with a defined role
A system that routes decisions back to you when they matter
Built on playbooks (directives) you can read and edit
Honest about confidence — it tells you when it's unsure
Extensible — new agents and skills can be added over time
Auditable — every action is logged and traceable
Where to Start as a New User
1️⃣
Pick one domain
Email, calendar, or a specific workflow. Don't try to build everything at once.
2️⃣
Write the directive
A plain-English SOP for what the agent should do. Start simple — 10 lines is enough.
3️⃣
Connect the tools
Authenticate Gmail, Calendar, or whatever the directive needs. One-time setup per tool.
4️⃣
Run it, learn, iterate
Run it once manually. See what breaks. Update the directive. The system gets smarter as you teach it.
