Morning Brief — Recommended Schema
Brief Structure
  • 1
    Priority Emails — unread, flagged as action-needed, summarized 1 line each
  • 2
    Today's Calendar — events in order, prep notes if available
  • 3
    Drafts Waiting — any replies drafted but not reviewed yet
  • 4
    VIP Watch — any message from priority contacts, regardless of label
  • 5
    Digest — newsletters / FYI threads summarized in bulk (optional)
Gaps to Address Before Building
Triage category schema — define his 6–8 email buckets live on the call
VIP contact list — who always surfaces, no matter what
Brief output destination — email-to-self or Notion, decide now
Draft vs. send policy — safety rail, must be explicit in directive
Voice annotation — batch analysis alone is weak; annotate 5 examples
Trigger schedule — when does this run? Morning cron or manual?
SCOPE RULE FOR THIS CALL
Don't build all three (voice + triage + brief) in one session. Pick one, get it working, leave him with a prototype he can actually use.

Email Labels — Suggested Starting Point
ACTION — needs reply today WAITING — sent, awaiting response MEETING — cal invites, scheduling BILLING — invoices, receipts, renewals VIP — from priority contacts NEWSLETTER — informational, no action ARCHIVE — handled, no follow-up URGENT — time-sensitive, any source
Customize these with him on the call. The names matter less than the logic — each label needs a clear rule: "what makes an email land here?"
